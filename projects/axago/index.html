Mainframe Modernization Strategy for a Global Insurance Group

Duration: 6-8 month strategic planning initiative

Role: Core Contributor ‚Äì Application Inventory, Target Architecture, Strategic Toolkit

Scope: 9 independently managed global entities, spanning multiple business lines and technology platforms

Focus Areas: Strategic alignment, modernization planning, repository creation, target architectures, tool evaluation, dashboarding, cost modeling, and business case formulation

Overview

A large multinational insurance group engaged us to define a unified, long-term modernization strategy for their mainframe landscape. The challenge stemmed from the fact that each of the nine regional entities operated independently ‚Äî with diverse technologies, business domains, and modernization readiness.

Each entity had a different mix of mainframe applications (COBOL, PL/I, DB2, IMS), varying levels of documentation and architecture maturity, and fragmented tooling preferences.

The Goal:

To develop a group-wide modernization playbook that respects local realities while driving global alignment, architecture reuse, and strategic acceleration across all entities ‚Äî and to articulate a compelling business case for change.

Business & Technical Problems

Category Challenge
Fragmented Visibility No central inventory or modernization view across entities
Technology Silos Diverse platforms and inconsistent architecture maturity
Tool Redundancy No shared tooling strategy; redundant evaluations at each entity
Unclear Prioritization Lack of an objective modernization readiness assessment
No Business Case No quantified view of benefits (time to market, TCO reduction, etc.)

Solution Approach

1. Centralized Application Repository

Built a validated, group-wide repository of mainframe and supporting applications

Metadata included:

  • Functional domain (Life, P&C, Health, etc.)
  • Technology stack (COBOL, DB2, PL/I, IMS, SAP, etc.)
  • Entity ownership, criticality, modernization urgency
  • Business Fit and Technical Fit scores via architect workshops

2. Strategic Dashboards & Risk Visualizations

Interactive Power BI dashboards presented:

  • Application distribution by fit, risk, platform type
  • Platform dependency heatmaps
  • Entity-wise modernization progress and exposure
  • Dashboards enabled CxO-level risk discussions and funding decisions

3. Business Case & Benefits Realization Modeling

Developed a quantitative business case for modernization:

  • Reduced time to market for new features
  • Lower TCO via cloud platforms and tooling standardization
  • Improved agility through decoupled services and faster test cycles
  • Enhanced talent availability by moving off aging skill stacks

4. Reference Architectures & Standardization

Defined standard cloud-native target architectures:

  • Event-driven Java services
  • Azure SQL Server / AWS Aurora DB targets
  • API-first integration and batch replacement using queues

Recommended platform-level replacements:

  • Replacing legacy Life insurance platforms with Guidewire
  • Legacy rating engines ‚Üí standardized microservices
  • Mapped R-patterns per application: Rehost, Refactor, Replace, Retire, etc.

5. Cost & Tooling Benchmarks

Created tooling recommendation matrices by modernization pattern

Cost ranges and effort estimates for:

  • Discovery, POC, migration, integration, testing, cloud hosting
  • Benchmarked vendor offerings, licensing models, and regional availability

Lessons Learned

  • Strategic modernization isn't just about technology ‚Äî it's about creating alignment across culture, priorities, and capability maturity.
  • Even the most independent entities are willing to adopt shared platforms if the value is clearly articulated.
  • Dashboards unlock executive action ‚Äî raw Excel lists rarely trigger funding decisions.
  • Tooling discussions must be backed by effort, supportability, and TCO ‚Äî not just feature checklists.
  • You need both bottom-up validation (architect workshops) and top-down vision (CxO strategy).
